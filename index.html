 About Me

I am a retired education (CSEA) Union Rep who has lived in Garden Grove District 5 for 12 years. I have lived in Orange County since 1966.

I currently work 3 part time jobs:

1. Union appointed, Personnel Commissioner with Garden Grove Unified School District
I oversee, with two other commissioners, the hiring, reclassification and discipline appeals of classified school employees (support staff) off and on for over 10 years.

2. I am the Chair of the Youth Council for the Santa Ana Workforce Development Board which helps train young people for real jobs and career pathways for over 10 years

3. I am currently the President of Chapman Commons Home Owners Association (HOA). 220 high rise condo units in District 5 near Chapman and Harbor Blvd.

Personal Experience

Before retirement, I was a classified school employee, Lead Custodian, in Huntington Beach Elementary School District for 25 years, where I became a Union/CSEA Chapter President and Chief Union Steward. I was also very politically active locally and advocate for social justice nationally.

Later I became an employee of the California School Employees Association as an education union Labor Relations Representative for 16 years culminating working in LAUSD for two years.During my time as a union rep I advocated for education friendly propositions in CA including Prop 30 Local Control Funding Formula, which moved more funding to students in need.

I am 65 years old. A Widower (6 years) from a 23 year relationship with Quan A. Nguyen.
Yes, I am an OUT Proud Queer American since 1975.

I enjoy collecting art, weight lifting, swimming and bicycling and being a local “foodie” enjoying the many restaurant food flavors from around the world offered in Garden Grove and surrounding Orange County.

City Budget, Public Safety and Crime Reduction

Did you know that 52% of the city budget goes to the police department alone?

I strongly believe in public safety but If elected I will encourage the Garden Grove City Council to begin to have a reasonable conversation with ALL stakeholders in our city, including the Garden Grove Police Officers Assoc.(GGPOA) about investing MORE in community support services. Public safety is more than cops on the street.. Most voters agree that adequate access to jobs, healthcare, and education as being crucial to a safe community. Council member indifference to constituents in Council District 5

Do you know the name of your current City Council person? I didn’t either. I looked her up on the GG City website for help on a routine matter. IT took many emails and an extended amount of time to get through to her. That wont ever happen if you elect me, Robert Tucker to District 5.
It is my opinion and experience that the current City Council District 5 person is indifferent to the needs of the constituents in District 5, especially Latinx and Asians. Well, frankly everyone.
My promise if elected is I will reach out to everyone and every business REGULARLY. I have the time and energy as a retiree to do so. I will seek your opinion, your voice on the matters that affect YOU.

There is no reason why District 5 shouldn’t become a destination for citizens of OC and tourists from abroad.I will work with the local chamber of commerce to come up with ways to not only make District 5 business thoroughfares more attractive, but I would seek ideas to truly make district 5 a destination. The Orange Circle or Main St should not be the only attractions in this area for OC Citizens. I will advocate for District 5 businesses right to their fair share of City revenues. Measure O monies should not be for the police alone.Fairness must prevail. A city council persons’ responsibility is to the entire community, not just the well monied few special interests.

If you give me your vote I promise I will engage all stakeholders in District 5.

COVID and Governments role in protecting us


COVID 19 Pandemic as we all know has changed everything in our lives in our Country. WE are extremely polarized and have unfortunately politicized this awful disease as we seek a rational road to recovery both health wise and economically.

No matter which political persuasion you are, I think we all may agree that government has failed us in our immense moment of need. Many folks don’t pay a whole lot of attention to government and politics BUT they do expect government to be there when there is a major disaster.

Government and our healthcare infrastructure was woefully unresponsive and unprepared. Many out of work people due to COVID thought they could count on the Employment Development Department to help them only to be horribly betrayed by this grossly incompetent bureaucracy.

Businesses in Garden Grove are closing and desperately need help. The state doled out large amounts of money to neighboring cities but not so much to Garden Grove.

Where was my District 5 reps voice? Why weren’t they “screaming” for help? Only silence.

I watched the cars lined up for hours around the block outside of the Orange County Labor Federation headquarters in Orange giving away food on many occasions to hungry maybe even starving citizens because THEIR GOVERNMENT FAILED THEM!!
Labor and its ginormous heart and caring for working families stepped up heroically to the plate and served the community. The City of Garden Grove? Well they have a nice section on their website discussing washing hands, oh and you can apply for a grant if you’re lucky.

If elected I assure you I will fight for you and our fair share of government bailout money in emergencies, and implementing science based health practices during this nightmare known as COVID 19.
