VOTE DAVID CROCKETT FOR RANCHO SANTIAGO COMMUNITY COLLEGE BOARD

As our community faces a global pandemic and a national reexamination of inequality and injustice, our community colleges can and must play a critical role in addressing these issues.

This is why I am running for the Rancho Santiago Community College District Board.

I personally benefitted from the affordable and quality education community colleges provide, and I am dedicated to ensuring that future generations have the same opportunities.

I attended Southern West Virginia Community College before transferring to and graduating from West Virginia University. My education prepared me to pursue a career in affordable healthcare management. 

My educational experience includes teaching with Jobs Corps, leading a school-to-work program and managing on-site courses for Peirce College.

On the Rancho Santiago Board I will:

  • Work to ensure our diverse student body has access to affordable and high-quality education that prepares them for a successful future.
  • Strengthen ties with the local business community by partnering on vocational training programs and internships. 
  • Advocate to expand financial assistants to students affected by COVID.
  • Secure the resources our educators to teach effectively online and reexamine learning environments.
The District’s colleges, Santa Ana College and Santiago Canyon College, are incredibly diverse and serve a variety of needs. Every year, thousands of students transfer to four-year institutions, future first responders train at Santa Ana College’s Fire Academy, and one-fifth of the students are over 35.

Whether you are a retiree committed to lifelong learning, a first-generation immigrant taking ESL classes, a working adult looking to gain technical skills or a recent high school graduate, my mission is to make the Rancho Santiago Community College District an affordable and quality institution for higher education that promotes personal and professional development. I hope to earn your vote.

David Crockett grew up in Matewan, West Virginia; a small town with one stop light and a significant place in history. For decades the Hatfield’s and McCoy’s feuded there in the 1800’s and in 1920 striking coal miners stood up for their rights and fought mine owners hired goons in the Battle of Matewan. David has kept the fighting spirit of his hometown with him.

He is one of thirteen children and from early on he has been a hard worker who began paying taxes at the age of 13. While still in High School he began classes at Southern West Virginia Community College.

He graduated from West VA University with a BA in English and has been committed to lifelong learning and teaching others ever since. He was a teacher with Job Corp in Harpers Ferry led a school-to-work program and managed on-site courses for Peirce College.

David has decades of experience as an advisor and manager providing affordable and quality healthcare primarily to Medicare and Medicaid recipients. His career took him and his family to Santa Ana 8 years ago and is passionately committed and active in his adopted hometown.
From the Million Man March to the Woman’s March, from Washington DC to Santa Ana, David is an activist who has took to the streets to protest for justice.
