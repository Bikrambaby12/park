Andre Charles is a political consultant with over 20 years of campaign experience in California. Over his career, he has consulted for candidates and ballot measures statewide and locally in Alameda, Contra Costa, Fresno, Los Angeles, Marin, Monterey, Napa, Orange, San Bernardino San Francisco, San Mateo, Santa Clara, Sonoma, and Ventura Counties.

Andre Charles

Founder

He has worked to elect candidates for City Council, School Board, Board of Supervisors, Special Districts, State Assembly, State Senate and Congress, and consulted for independent expenditure campaigns, local land use measures, school bonds, transportation bonds and dedicated sales taxes across the state.

In 2018, Andre Charles served as the lead consultant for a statewide ballot measure (the College for All Act), District Attorney campaigns in Orange and Marin Counties, and State Senate & Assembly races in Southern California. Charles is currently the Data & Political Director for Flip the West, a Super PAC that helped flip 7 of the 14 Republican held Congressional Districts in California last cycle, and he was honored in 2019 by the American Association of Political Consultants for Best Local Mail nationwide.

The services we offer you

From the moment you throw you hat into the ring to when you give your victory speech on Election Night, Andre Charles Consulting will support your campaign with uniquely tailored, high-quality campaign services. Andre Charles has a proven track record in all facets of political organizing, including but not limited to:

Creating and implementing paid and earned media outreach strategies.

Developing and executing detailed campaign budgets, plans, and timelines.

Drafting key messages and talking points based on input from the candidate, key stakeholders and polling information.

Writing and designing persuasive political mailers and campaign materials.

Working with an extensive, experienced network of vendors (printers, mail houses, paid phone banks, etc.) to produce campaign materials.

Maintaining voter and contributor databases.


Planning and implementing “get out the vote” activities.

Researching and analyzing legislative and election legal issues.

Responding promptly to media and constituent questions.

Building coalitions of community leaders and elected officials.

Recruiting, training, and retaining volunteer networks.


Planning and hosting fundraising and other campaign events.
