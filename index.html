ABOUT

LORDES EVERETT

Lourdes Everett was raised in a California when higher education and housing was affordable. Because of these opportunities, she was able to overcome early challenges and today she is a Democratic Party activist, an advocate for civil liberties as a Board Member of the ACLU, a former first responder and a mother of three.

Lourdes running for Assembly to ensure the next generation of Californians enjoy the same opportunities she had.
Lourdes faced early obstacles with determination and perseverance.

As a teenager she ran away from an abusive home and had her first child at 17. She became a single mother due to gun violence at 19. Without our affordable community college system, she would not have been able to become an emergency first responder and raise a family.

Lourdes never stopped expanding her educational and professional horizons. Late in life, Lourdes graduated from Law School Cum Laude. Her family also grew as she married her now belated husband Terry, a Vietnam veteran and proud Union Steward.

Now that her children are grown and starting their own families Lourdes wants to bring the tenacity that took her through tough times to Sacramento.

MY LIFE

In my words

“You never really know a man until you stand in his shoes and walk around in them”, my favorite excerpt from a novel written by Harper Lee “To Kill A Mockingbird.” Being raised in California, I have seen many walks-of- life, from people that had a tremendous amount of money and those who barely got by. I was fortunate to be able to walk in both shoes, where I lived in great comfort, and on the opposite; I lived extremely poor, with the bare minimum and sometimes with none.

Growing up, I lived well, middle class; however, subjected to abuse, I ran away. I lived from place to place and at one point, even slept in a car. I had my first child at 17 and by 19, I had two daughters and was pregnant with the third. During this period, we lost my daughters father through gun violence when he was shot and killed in front of me. Ending up on welfare to feed and keep a roof over my daughters’ heads, I went to college in hopes of getting a decent job. I was accepted and enrolled into the LVN program, later becoming a certified EMT, where I worked as a First Responder for over 10 years while I continued to earn college credit units. Receiving my license to practice Real Estate, I helped my late husband Terry established our Real Estate Company, which I continue to run today and finally in my later years I earned my Juris Doctorate Degree.

I believe in equality and in an equitable system. Since my early 20s I have advocated for Better Living Conditions to ease the struggles of life in the frontlines and behind the lines, I decided it is time to take my advocacy for the people to the inside. This is why I am running for Assembly.
